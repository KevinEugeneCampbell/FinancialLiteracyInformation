Loans

In this page we will be learning about what loans are, the different types of loans and amortization schedule.

  • Principal: amount of money borrowed from lender
  • Interest: fee that you pay to the lender for borrowing money
  • Repayment period: length of time that you have to repay the loan
  • Monthly payment: amount of money that you pay to the lender each month to repay the loan
  • Grace period: period of time after you take a loan that you do not have to make a payment
  • Collateral: asset that you pledge to the lender in case you default on the loan
  • Default: situation where you fail to make a payment on a loan
  • Debt-to-income ratio: measure of your financial health that compares your monthly debt payments to your monthly income
  • Lien: legal claim that a lender has on your property to secure a loan

What are Loans? How do you apply for it?
A loan is a trasnfer of money by one party to another with an agreement to pay it back. The borrower usually incurs a debt and is usually required to pay interest for the use of the money. To apply for a loan you will need to provide the lender with information about your income, assets, and debt. The lender will then use this info to assess your eligibility for the loan and what interest rate should be charged. Before you sign any loan documents, it is important to understand the terms of the loan, including the interest rate, repayment period, and fees.

What is an Amortization Schedule?
It is a table that shows how much of your monthly loan payment goes towards principal and interest, and how much of the loan you will have paid off over time. It is a useful tool for understanding how a loan works and for budgeting your monthly payments. The things included in a Amortization schedule include: loan amount, interest rate, number of payments, monthly payment, principal paid each month, interest paid each month and remaining balance.
