Credit

Through this you will be learning what Credit, Credit Scores, Credit Cards, and APR is.
What is credit score?
Credit score is a number that lenders use to assess your creditworthiness based on your credit report. The higher score you posess the higher your creditworthiness. Here are the general credit score ranges:

  • 800-850: Excellent Credit Score
  • 740-799: Very Good Credit Score
  • 670-739: Good Credit Score
  • 580-669: Fair Credit Score
  • 300-579: Poor Credit Score
There are three main credit bureaus which collect credit history an assess creditworthiness. Experien, Equifax, and TransUnion are these credit bureaus, lenders will buy data from these bureaus for loan decsions. If you ever want to dispute an error with your credit score, the credit bureaus is where you would go. Access a free credit score report from here: AnnualCreditReport.com
What are Credit Cards? Credit Cards is a mysterious slim rectangular shapped object which you can use to borrow money to pay for goods and services. On every card there is Credit max which allows the user to borrow this much money, along with the credit max there is also a minimum payment that follows every month to the card. If you do not pay this minimum payment towards this card it will start collecting interest and could lead to credit card debt. Moving on, there are also many different type of credit cards that allow for different things. Make sure to get in contact with an financial advisor, one from a fiduciary is preferred.
What is Annual Percentage Rate? (APR) It is basically a yearly percentage of borrowing money, including interest and fees. Lower APR = less interest over time. One thing to note is that the lender must disclose the APR, if you do not see it anywhere when getting a loan make sure to ask.
